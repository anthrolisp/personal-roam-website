  • Notes::

    • The stated goal of the paper is for accurate prediction of traffic flow.
      • Making estimations of the future state based on expereinces and knowledge extracted from related historical data
    • The historical data matters the most. Recent advances in traffic data acquisition technology has given researchers a traffic data flood.
    • At the time of the articles publishing, there was work in using neural networks for traffic forecasting, but the authors raise the concern of traffic flow being complicated, with plentiful characteristics in both space and time dimensions.
      • "It is not an easy task to develop a deep learning model that efficiently capture the characteristics of traffic flow."
    • The more critical question in addition to how to model is how to understand the data-driven prediction methods
      • "In the transportation field, data-driven methods especially the neural network approach have long been criticized for its explain-ability. Neural network has long been regarded as a “black box” because it is difficult to understand due to the large number of neurons, complicated structures, and non-linear function (Karlaftis and Vlahogianni, 2011, Lippi et al., 2013, Zhang et al., 2014)."

    • What's new about this paper?

      • The authors transition to using two different neural networks:
        • A deep convolutional neural network is used for the spatial features of the network
        • Recurrent neural netwoks were used for the temporal data
          • The time horizon was up recent horizon plus similar intervals of the previous day and previous week
        • Speed data was used to train an "attention model" to deal with the level of importance of recent traffic
        • The proposed model was evaluated on the prediction of future traffic flow on a long future horizon
          • 45
        • They also analyzed the inner workings of the trained neural network.
    • neural networks handle non-linearities well, as well as approximating unknown functions. It is natural to apply it to traffic prediction
    • "Plain" neural networks suffer from their shallow-ness in the traffic space, where the input data is "deep"
      • deep neural networks to the rescue!
    • Traffic flow is similar to video and audio in that is has characteristics in both the space and time domainds
      • traffic flow patterns at one location are more likely to be similar to another location

    • Experimental Setup:

      • The real-world traffic data is aggregated into 5 minute intervals
      • The data made available to the neural networks is as follows:
        • Say it is 6:30PM, then we want to predict 6:35, 6:45, ..., till 7:15pm .
        • To predict these 45 minutes of traffic, the model looks back
          • 105 minutes in the current day,
          • 30 minutes before and after 6:30PM on the previous day
          • 30 minutes before and after 6:30PM t-1 week
      • The network:
        • It is a corridor of Interstate 405, considering only the Northbound direction
          • 33 detectors, data from PeMS

    • Results

      • This paper's DNN-BTF model was compared with several different methods
        • DNN-BTF stands for Deep Neural Network Traffic Flow prediction model. Not sure where the B comes from...
      • The attention model, which weights the inputs by importance, understandably gave more importance to recent time intervals.
      • The spatial weighting was less predictable
      • Their visualization of the inner workings of the model is super well done
      • Model accuracy is given but not clearly communicated
